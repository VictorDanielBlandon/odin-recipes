How to Make Honduran Baleadas: A guide for lazy people with little motivation (like me!)

A typical Honduran Baleada, popular among toursits.

The Honduran "Baleada" is the unofficial national dish of the Republic of Honduras.

Most often confused by toursits as a "burrito", this dish differs from your typical californian dish by being the only vegetarian friendly option in a sea of meat-filled dishes in this Central American nation.

Ingredients:

  • A pack of flour tortillas
  • An avocado
  • 2-3 eggs
  • Re-fried beans
  • Fried plantains
  • Honduran Mantequilla

Steps:

    The steps part assumes you have a pan and a stove, with at least a spatula.

  1. First, start your stove to medium heat, place your pan and let it heat for about 3 minutes.
  2. Then add one flour tortilla first and heat each side for at least 2 minutes (less if you see it necessary).
  3. When you feel your tortillas warm and toasty, place them aside for a while.
  4. Make scrambled eggs and season them with salt and pepper.
  5. After your eggs are done, place the eggs in a seperate serving plate.
  6. Open your refried bean bag or can and cook them in a stove, after 5 minutes of adding water and cooking, place them in a seperate serving bowl.
  7. Place the already prepared fried plantains and Mantequilla into seperate bowls too.
  8. After preparing all of the toppings start assembling your baleadas.
  9. Lay some refried bean spread into a tortilla and start laying your ingredients one by one, making sure to leave the mantequilla as the last topping.
  10. B e sure to enjoy!
